Overview

My research explores lexical (word) and semantic (meaning) representations and the interface of these systems across different languages. This work includes bilinguals at all levels of proficiency as well as people who are just starting to learn a language. To this end, I use traditional behavioral methods, computational simulations, and neuroimaging technology (such as MRI and near-infrared spectroscopy). My work explores the highly dynamic and interactive cognitive and neural systems that support the representation linguistic knowledge in humans who speak one, two, or many languages.

For more information, visit the Raizada Lab website or view my detailed pages below.
